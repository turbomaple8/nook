Moving to London for a Grad Scheme: Your Housing Timeline

From offer letter to keys in hand — without paying August rent on a September job.

Most London grad schemes start in September, which means every August the same scramble repeats: thousands of new graduates hunting rooms in the same four weeks. The fix isn't luck — it's a timeline. Here's the month-by-month version, working back from a September start.

June–July: Decide Your Strategy (You Are Here)

Two workable strategies. Strategy A: book ahead. Providers with fixed room stock — co-living operators, PBSA — let you reserve a specific room for a September date months out, often from abroad with a video viewing. You lock certainty; you commit early. Strategy B: land and hunt. Take a short-term stay for your first two weeks, then flat-hunt in person. More choice, more stress, and August/September is peak-competition season — expect same-day decisions.

Budget first either way: on a typical £32–38k grad salary, take-home is roughly £2,100–£2,400/month. The old "a third on rent" rule stretches to 40–45% in London; an all-inclusive room from £215/week (subject to availability) keeps you at the sane end of that. Numbers explained in our intern & grad housing guide.

Early August: Lock the Essentials

Booking ahead? Reserve now — September stock at fair prices thins out by mid-August. Confirm in writing: start date, what's included, deposit amount and protection scheme. UK deposits can legally reach five weeks' rent; two weeks (Nook's standard) leaves £800+ in your pocket on a typical room.

Hunting on arrival? Book your buffer accommodation now instead — two weeks, cancellable. Line up viewings for your first 72 hours before you land; good rooms go in a day.

Late August: Paperwork Week

Right to Rent documents (passport, visa/share code), proof of your grad scheme offer, and references if asked. No UK guarantor? Sorted options exist — we covered them in housing without a UK guarantor. Set up a UK bank account early (app-based banks take minutes); some landlords want a UK account for the standing order.

September Week 1: Move In, Not Move Around

Your first week of a grad scheme is intense — training, names, new city. The single best gift you can give week-1 you is a room that already works: bed made, WiFi on, bills handled, flatmates who know where the good coffee is. That's the case for arriving into a ready home rather than a hunt. Where to live near Canary Wharf if that's where your desk is.

The One Mistake to Avoid

Signing a 12-month tenancy in a panic during week one. Grad schemes rotate — some rotations change city — and breaking a fixed tenancy costs real money. Flexible contracts from three months exist precisely for this. Keep your options open until you know your rotation map.

September start confirmed? Browse rooms you can book from abroad — video viewings, online contracts, keys on landing day.
